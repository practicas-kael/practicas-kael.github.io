👤 1. Personal Pronouns - Los Pronombres Personales

Los pronombres personales son palabras que usamos en lugar de los nombres de las personas, animales o cosas.

Los Pronombres Personales:

I
yo
You
tú / usted
He
él
She
ella
It
eso (animales/cosas)
We
nosotros/nosotras
They
ellos/ellas
📝 Ejemplos:
• I am Emi. (Yo soy Emi)
• You are my friend. (Tú eres mi amigo/a)
• He is my brother. (Él es mi hermano)
• She is my sister. (Ella es mi hermana)
• It is a dog. (Es un perro)
• We are students. (Nosotros somos estudiantes)
• They are happy. (Ellos están felices)
💡 Recuerda:
• I siempre se escribe con mayúscula (aunque no esté al principio de la oración)
• He = para niños, hombres y animales machos
• She = para niñas, mujeres y animales hembras
• It = para cosas, objetos, animales cuando no sabemos si son macho o hembra
• You = puede ser tú (singular) o ustedes (plural)

✨ 2. Verb "to Be" - El Verbo Ser/Estar

El verbo "to be" es el más importante en inglés. Lo usamos para decir quiénes somos, cómo estamos, dónde estamos, etc.

Las Formas del Verbo "to Be":

am
soy / estoy
is
es / está
are
eres / estás / son / están

¿Cuándo usar cada forma?

Pronombre Verbo "to Be" Ejemplo
I (yo) am I am happy. (Yo estoy feliz)
You (tú/usted/ustedes) are You are smart. (Tú eres inteligente)
He (él) is He is tall. (Él es alto)
She (ella) is She is kind. (Ella es amable)
It (eso) is It is a cat. (Es un gato)
We (nosotros) are We are friends. (Somos amigos)
They (ellos/ellas) are They are students. (Ellos son estudiantes)
📝 Más Ejemplos:
• I am 10 years old. (Tengo 10 años)
• You are my best friend. (Eres mi mejor amigo/a)
• He is in the classroom. (Él está en el salón)
• She is very smart. (Ella es muy inteligente)
• It is sunny today. (Está soleado hoy)
• We are ready. (Estamos listos)
• They are playing. (Ellos están jugando)
💡 Regla Fácil:
• I → siempre usa am
• He, She, It → siempre usan is
• You, We, They → siempre usan are

Formas Cortas (Contractions):

Forma Larga Forma Corta

📝 Ejemplos con Formas Cortas:

💡 How to use it:
• It's sunny. (Está soleado.)
• It's cloudy. (Está nublado.)
• It's rainy. (Está lluvioso.)
• It's windy. (Está ventoso.)
• It's cold. (Está frío.)
📖 Examples:
• Question: "What's the weather like?"
• Answer: "It's sunny and hot." (Está soleado y caliente.)

• Question: "How is the weather in San José?"
• Answer: "It's cloudy in San José." (Está nublado en San José.)

Subject + am/is/are + wearing + article + adjective + clothing

🌤️ 5. Weather and Appropriate Clothing

¿Qué ropa usar según el clima? (What clothing to wear according to the weather?)

Sunny Day ☀️

What to wear: t-shirt, shorts, sunglasses, hat, flip flops, swimsuit

📖 "Today is a sunny day. I use a blue pants, yellow t-shirt, and green hat."

Rainy Day 🌧️

What to wear: raincoat, umbrella, boots

📖 "It's rainy today. I am wearing a raincoat, boots and I have an umbrella."

Cold/Snowy Day ❄️

What to wear: coat, sweater, scarf, gloves, mittens, hat, boots

📖 "It's cold and snowy. I am wearing a warm coat, a scarf, gloves and boots."

Windy Day 💨

What to wear: sweater, pants, jacket

📖 "It's windy. I am wearing a sweater and pants."
💡 Important: We choose our clothing based on the weather to be comfortable!
(¡Elegimos nuestra ropa según el clima para estar cómodos!)

🇨🇷 6. Weather in Costa Rica

Different cities in Costa Rica have different weather conditions:

Cities and their typical weather:

  • San José: It's cloudy in San José (Está nublado en San José)
  • Alajuela: It's stormy in Alajuela (Está tormentoso en Alajuela)
  • Cartago: It's cold in Cartago (Está frío en Cartago)
  • Guanacaste: It's hot and sunny (Está caliente y soleado)
  • Limón: It's rainy (Está lluvioso)
  • Puntarenas: It's sunny (Está soleado)
📖 Conversation Example:

Person 1: "What's the weather like in San José?"
Person 2: "It is cloudy in San José."

Person 1: "What's the weather like in Alajuela?"
Person 2: "It's stormy in Alajuela."

❓ 7. Common Questions and Answers

About Weather:

  • Q: What's the weather like? → A: It's sunny/cloudy/rainy
  • Q: How is the weather? → A: It is hot/cold/windy
  • Q: What's the weather like in [city]? → A: It's [weather] in [city]

About Seasons:

  • Q: What's your favorite season? → A: I love winter/summer/spring/fall
  • Q: What's your least favorite season? → A: I don't like fall/winter

About Clothing:

  • Q: What are you wearing? → A: I am wearing a blue dress
  • Q: What are you wearing today? → A: I am wearing pants and a t-shirt
💡 Remember:
• Use "It is" or "It's" for weather
• Use "I am" or "I'm" for what you're wearing
• Adjectives go BEFORE the noun: a blue shirt (not "a shirt blue")
